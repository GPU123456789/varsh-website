Araiyar Sevai , is a Tamil Hindu performing art form, centered on the ritual singing and enactment of the hymns of the Divya Prabandham, the canon of the Sri Vaishnava tradition. Araiyar Sevai is generally performed only inside Vaishnavite temples in the presence of the temple utsavar — the temple's processional deity — by hereditary male performers called Araiyars.[1] Although temple inscriptions suggest its performance was once widespread, it is today only performed in few temples in Tamil Nadu: the temple shrine of Srirangam, shrine of Azhagiya Manavala Perumal Temple, the shrine of Alvar Tirunagari and the temple of Andal at Srivilliputhur. It is also performed in a different form at the Melkote temple in southern Karnataka, where the hymns of the Divya Prabhandham is sung, but not enacted..

M. Varshini is a passionate student of classical arts, currently pursuing a Diploma in Bharatanatyam and a B.A. in English at the University of Madras. With a deep appreciation for Tamil culture and heritage, she is devoted to promoting Ariyar Sevai — the ancient temple tradition that unites music, dance, and devotion in the recitation of the Nalayira Divya Prabandham.

Connection to Ariyar Sevai Varshin draws inspiration from the roots of Ariyar Sevai, a divine art form that beautifully blends expression (abhinaya), melody, and spirituality — elements also central to Bharatanatyam. She views it as a living bridge between temple worship and classical performance, reflecting the spiritual essence of Tamil Vaishnava tradition. Through her 4th year dissertaion, and outreach efforts, Varshini strives to create awareness about Ariyar Sevai among younger generations. She works to highlight its historical significance and artistic depth, encouraging its revival and recognition as a cornerstone of Tamil devotional heritage. By blending her Bharatanatyam training and literary insight, she aims to present Ariyar Sevai as not just a ritual, but a cultural expression of divine devotion that continues to inspire modern audiences.
